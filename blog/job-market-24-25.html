2025-05-02

The 2024-25 job market experience

Humbling.

As I write this, I still haven't landed a full-time role yet. I've finished university, completed my submissions, and now it's just a waiting game, hoping to hear back from someone, at least an interview, but nothing. I used to think applying for placements during 22-23 was rough, but looking back, that was manageable. This is something entirely different.

The mentality needed to survive this is to be blindly optimistic. That's honestly the only thing you can rely on, to stay completely positive and just keep going, trying every possible route to land a job. I feel like I might've hit my limit, but I won't let it get to me. God, please give me strength to stay on my toes and keep fighting this market. Maybe too dramatic, but it is what it is.

As for the numbers, I've sent out around 300-400 applications, completed so many OA's, video interviews, but never made it to the final rounds. Just two face-to-face interviews. Hundreds of rejections. I swear I'm not trying to sound negative, but this is just the reality so far. And somehow, despite all this, I still wake up and apply, hoping to find that one right job.

On the other side, I'm also hunting for temporary part-time jobs, and even with my experience in the hospitality sector, I can't seem to land anything. Feels like every direction I turn is a closed door. What's happening? How are things supposed to turn around in the future? It's too much to stress over, and I really don't want to imagine the worst.

All of this makes a person start doubting themselves, whether they're capable of doing anything at all. Makes you question if all the effort you've been putting in was for nothing. And I genuinely feel for others going through this too. I can't even properly wrap up this blog because my search is still ongoing.
